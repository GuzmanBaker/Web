Why we're Different


We're committed to providing accessible, high-quality mental health care for the whole family. Our therapists are specially trained to bring unconscious patterns into your conscious awareness. As you become aware of what was previously unconscious to you, we help you interrupt these patterns and relate to them with a sense of empowered agency. While active listening and empathy are essential, we recognize that meaningful and enduring change comes from challenging you to think and exist differently with feedback that is compassionate, honest, and actionable. One of the many things that sets us apart from most therapists is that we provide practical skills that allow you to take tangible action steps to overcome your mental health challenges and reach your goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are all therapy sessions conducted online, or do you provide in-person appointments as well?

Currently, the majority of our sessions are conducted online using a secure, HIPAA-compliant telehealth platform designed to protect your privacy. In-person appointments may be available, by arrangement, in select community locations such as local libraries. These options are considered on a case-by-case basis — please speak with your clinician to discuss whether in-person sessions are possible for your situation

How do I schedule an appointment, and how quickly can I be seen for my first session?

To begin your journey with us, simply complete our new patient request form and indicate your clinician preference. You’re welcome to select a specific therapist or opt for our team’s assistance in recommending the best fit for your needs. While we do our best to honor clinician requests, please note that not all therapists are trained to address every concern. After submitting your request, our administrative team will reach out within 48 hours to review your information and guide you through the next steps in the process.

What types of therapy modalities do your providers offer (e.g., CBT, EMDR, TF-CBT, group therapy)?

Our clinicians are trained in a broad range of evidence-based therapeutic modalities, with each therapist offering specific expertise to best support your unique needs. Examples of available approaches include Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR), Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT), Parent-Child Interaction Therapy (PCIT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), Person-Centered Therapy (PCT), Compassion-Focused Therapy (CMT), and Behavior Modification therapy.

How do you determine which therapy approach is right for me or my child?

During your initial session, your clinician will collaborate with you to set meaningful therapy goals based on your unique needs. Your therapist will then draw upon the most appropriate evidence-based modalities, carefully tailoring treatment approaches to match your presenting concerns and ensure you receive highly effective care using evidence based practices.

I don't see my insurance listed above, how does out-of-network billing work and will I have out-of-pocket costs?

We are in-network providers with the insurance carriers listed above. As a courtesy, Guzman & Baker Behavioral Health Services will verify your insurance benefits and provide an estimate of your out-of-pocket costs prior to your appointment. Please remember that it is ultimately your responsibility to understand your insurance coverage and any associated limitations. Guzman & Baker Behavioral Health Services does not directly bill insurance for out-of-network benefits. However, after your session, we can provide you with a superbill—a detailed receipt of services—that you may submit to your insurance company for possible partial reimbursement, depending on your plan’s out-of-network coverage.
